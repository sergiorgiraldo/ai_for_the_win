# Responsible Use Policy

This document outlines the intended use, prohibited uses, and legal considerations for the AI for the Win training materials.

## Purpose and Intended Audience

This training program is designed exclusively for:

- **Security professionals** learning to build AI-powered defensive tools
- **Students and researchers** studying AI applications in cybersecurity
- **Blue team practitioners** improving threat detection and incident response capabilities
- **Educators** teaching AI and security concepts in authorized settings

## Permitted Uses

You may use these materials to:

- Learn AI/ML techniques for security applications
- Build defensive security tools for authorized environments
- Conduct security research in controlled, authorized settings
- Teach or present in educational contexts (with attribution)
- Adapt and build upon for non-commercial educational purposes

## Prohibited Uses

You may NOT use these materials to:

- **Conduct unauthorized attacks** against systems you do not own or have explicit permission to test
- **Develop malware** or offensive tools intended for malicious purposes
- **Perform social engineering** attacks against real individuals or organizations without authorization
- **Bypass security controls** in production environments without proper authorization
- **Harvest credentials** or sensitive data from systems you do not own
- **Sell or commercially distribute** without obtaining a commercial license (see LICENSE)
- **Remove attribution** when sharing or adapting the materials
- **Train AI models** on this content for commercial purposes without authorization

## Legal Compliance

Users are responsible for:

1. **Obtaining proper authorization** before testing any techniques on systems
2. **Complying with local laws** regarding computer access, penetration testing, and security research
3. **Following responsible disclosure** practices if vulnerabilities are discovered
4. **Respecting intellectual property** rights of third parties

### Relevant Laws (Non-exhaustive)

- **United States**: Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA), state-specific laws
- **European Union**: GDPR, NIS2 Directive, national computer crime laws
- **United Kingdom**: Computer Misuse Act 1990
- **International**: Budapest Convention on Cybercrime
