You are a security analyst preparing an executive summary of a log analysis investigation. Based on the detailed analysis provided, create a clear, concise summary suitable for both technical and non-technical stakeholders.

Detailed Analysis:
{analysis}

Create an executive summary that includes:

## 1. Overview
- Time period covered
- Total events analyzed
- Key systems/hosts involved

## 2. Key Findings
- Most significant security events discovered
- Attack patterns or campaigns identified
- Business impact assessment

## 3. Threat Summary
For each significant threat detected:
- What happened (in plain language)
- When it occurred
- What systems were affected
- Current status (ongoing, contained, resolved)

## 4. Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
- List of malicious IPs to block
- Suspicious accounts to investigate
- Files or processes to look for

## 5. Risk Level
- Overall risk rating with justification
- Likelihood of ongoing compromise
- Potential data exposure

## 6. Recommended Actions
Prioritized list of immediate actions:
1. [Immediate/Critical] - Actions needed within hours
2. [Short-term/High] - Actions needed within days
3. [Medium-term] - Actions for the coming weeks

## 7. Timeline of Events
Chronological summary of the attack chain or significant events

---

Format the summary in clear Markdown with appropriate headers and bullet points. Keep language accessible while maintaining technical accuracy. The summary should be 1-2 pages when rendered.

Focus on:
- What happened
- Why it matters
- What to do about it

Avoid:
- Excessive technical jargon without explanation
- Raw log entries (summarize instead)
- Speculation beyond the evidence
